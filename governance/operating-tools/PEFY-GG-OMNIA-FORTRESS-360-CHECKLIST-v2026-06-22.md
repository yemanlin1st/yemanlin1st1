## 1. Use Case

Use this checklist before finalizing any major document, platform, repository, proposal, codebase, design asset, governance pack, training material, client deliverable, partnership document, or strategic output.

---

## 2. Intake Check

- The real objective is clear.
- The audience is identified.
- The expected output format is known.
- The level of urgency is understood.
- The context, constraints, and success criteria are captured.
- Missing information is either resolved or clearly stated.

---

## 3. Confidentiality and Public-Safe Check

- The content is classified: Public, Internal, Restricted, or Confidential.
- Public/private information is separated.
- Public materials use coded ownership where relevant.
- Restricted operational values are excluded from public content.
- Client-specific or partner-specific sensitive information is not exposed.
- Private ownership mapping stays outside public repositories.

---

## 4. Strategy and Value Check

- The output supports a clear business or institutional purpose.
- It creates reusable asset value.
- It protects PEFY-GG strategic authority where relevant.
- It does not create uncontrolled dependency.
- It avoids giving away control, IP, clients, data, or future revenue rights.
- It can be reused, scaled, white-labeled, or adapted where useful.

---

## 5. Legal and IP Check

- Ownership is clear.
- Usage rights are clear.
- Licensing logic is clear where relevant.
- Confidentiality duties are visible where relevant.
- Non-circumvention logic is considered for partnerships.
- Subcontracting is controlled where relevant.
- Source code, data, documents, and derivative works are protected.
- Termination and post-project responsibilities are not forgotten.

---

## 6. Cyber, Data, and Technology Check

- Access control is considered.
- Data collection is minimized and justified.
- Privacy notices are considered where relevant.
- Audit trails or logs are considered where useful.
- Backup and recovery logic is considered.
- Technical choices are explainable.
- The solution is maintainable and modular.
- Deployment instructions are present where relevant.

---

## 7. Financial and Commercial Check

- Revenue model is clear where relevant.
- Cost responsibilities are defined.
- Payment milestones are clear where applicable.
- Margin protection is considered.
- Mission, travel, implementation, and support costs are not hidden.
- Revenue sharing does not weaken ownership or strategic control.
- Investor comfort is preserved where relevant.

---

## 8. Operational Check

- Roles and responsibilities are clear.
- Key steps are actionable.
- Timeline or milestones are defined where relevant.
- Evidence and proof logic are considered.
- KPIs or success indicators are defined where useful.
- Risks have owners or treatment actions.
- Handover and continuity are considered.

---

## 9. Editorial and Humanization Check

- Language is professional and natural.
- Unnecessary wording is removed.
- Repetition is reduced.
- Structure is easy to follow.
- The output is usable by the target audience.
- Claims are sober and evidence-aware.
- Formatting supports reading, review, and execution.

---

## 10. Failure-Aware Red Flag Check

Revise before release if the output contains:

- Ownership ambiguity.
- Unclear revenue sharing.
- Hidden exclusivity.
- Weak IP protection.
- Weak confidentiality protection.
- Undefined mission funding.
- Uncontrolled subcontracting.
- Weak dispute or termination logic.
- Unsupported compliance or certification claims.
- Weak technical feasibility.
- Poor user experience.
- Missing public/private classification.
- Excessive filler or generic language.

---
